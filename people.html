Principal Investigator

I joined UTSA’s Developmental and Regenerative Sciences program within the Department of Neuroscience, Developmental and Regenerative Biology as an Assistant Professor in Fall 2023. Prior to that, I was an NIH Pathway to Independence postdoctoral fellow in the Developmental Biology Program at the Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center in New York and obtained my Ph.D. in Evolutionary/Population genomics from the University of Rochester, NY. I began to use Drosophila as a model to study germline genetic conflicts (between transposable elements and the piwi-interacting RNA pathway) as a graduate student—a theme which continued during my postdoctoral work, where I expanded my studies to another class of selfish genes called meiotic drive genes and how their selfish activities are suppressed by the endogenous RNAi pathway. At UTSA, our work on understanding germline genetic conflicts continues with the Drosophila model, and in addition, we use bioinformatics approaches to examine genome sequence data from vertebrate model organisms such as mice to understand how selfish genes shape genome evolution.
